Why we built this

There’s a version of work where you’re always almost caught up. I know it well.

By day I run technology for a boarding school — one person for a full campus of hundreds of devices, hundreds of cadets, and nearly a hundred staff. What used to be a three-person team is now me. Off the clock, I coach Brazilian Jiu-Jitsu, host a podcast for the cadets I mentor, and try to be a present husband and father of four.

For years my plan was the one a lot of capable people run: start earlier, stay later, carry it all. It works until it doesn’t — until every hour you give to one thing is borrowed from another, and the people you love get the leftovers.

I didn’t need a motivational quote. I needed my time back.

So I built AI teammates for the real weight of my work. They took pieces off my plate so I could think, breathe, and show up again. They worked. And when something works that well on a problem that personal, you stop keeping it to yourself.

That’s Nakara™. We build AI employees so owners and operators can stop drowning in work that never ends — starting with Naka, a front desk that answers your phone and website and hands you a clean picture of what happened while you were heads-down.

This isn’t about replacing human care. It’s about protecting it.

Win back the hours.
Keep the promises that matter.
